## Precise novelty claim

The defensible claim is not “nobody has ever used AI for hospital shortages.” Public products already address shortage prediction, inventory, operations, drug information, pumps, and interoperability.

The distinctive integration demonstrated here is:

> a provenance-bound institutional change compiler that turns a mixed hospital corpus into one typed, synchronized, patient-path-tested, reversible change package; preserves independent specialist dissent; stages a fictional no-API legacy target; and technically prevents autonomous final commit.

This integration is more specific—and more credible—than an absolute world-first claim. If a materially similar public product is found, this table must be updated and the claim narrowed before submission.

## Why a simple chatbot does not reproduce it

A chatbot can propose prose. RENKEVIA must satisfy machine-checkable invariants: strict Patch IR, expected-before checks, six deterministic projections, stable evidence identities, 96 repeatable assertions, exact rollback hashes, independent review completeness, explicit blockers, and `finalCommitAllowed=false`. Fluency cannot set those states.
